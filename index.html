About Me

👋 Hi! I’m a fourth year Ph.D. candidate in EECS at U.C. Berkeley advised by Dr. Niloufar Salehi. My research applies human-computer interaction (HCI) methods to study machine learning and data systems. I’m currently thinking about how we can build trustworthy systems by aligning modeling assumptions with real world needs and goals, and prioritizing user agency. My current projects include developing more reliable machine translation systems for high-stakes domains like healthcare, and exploring what participant engagement might look like in the context of large-scale menstrual health research.

At Berkeley, I’m a member of BAIR, the Berkeley HCI Group and the EPIC Data Lab. During Summer 2022, I was an ML Research Intern on the Visualization team at Apple, supervised by Dr. Fred Hohman and Dr. Mary Beth Kery.
