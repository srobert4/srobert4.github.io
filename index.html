About Me

I am a first-year Ph.D. student in EECS at UC Berkeley. I graduated with a B.S. in Mathematical and Computational Science from Stanford University in June 2019. My research is at the intersection of machine learning and human-computer interaction. I am interested in the challenges that arise when deploying data-driven products with real users, including responsible data analytics, algorithmic fairness and transparency, data privacy, and AI ethics. In my free time you might find me at the beach (when it’s sunny), cooking or drawing (when it’s not), and occasionally practising my Tidyverse skills on fun datasets. I am very grateful to be supported by an EECS Excellence Award.
